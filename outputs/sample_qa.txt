QUESTION: What is the difference between Optima Secure and Optima Super Secure plans? Give complete details.
ANSWER: The Optima Secure and Optima Super Secure plans are both health insurance products offered by my: Optima Secure. Here are the differences between the two plans:

1. Base Sum Insured: The Base Sum Insured for Optima Secure is ₹10 lakh, while for Optima Super Secure, it is ₹15 lakh.

2. Secure Benefit: Optima Secure offers a Secure Benefit of ₹10 lakh, while Optima Super Secure offers a Secure Benefit of ₹20 lakh.

3. Cumulative Bonus / Plus Benefit: The Cumulative Bonus / Plus Benefit for Optima Secure is ₹10 lakh, while for Optima Super Secure, it is ₹10 lakh.

4. Automatic Restore Benefit: Both Optima Secure and Optima Super Secure offer an Automatic Restore Benefit of ₹10 lakh.

5. Maximum permissible amount for a single hospitalization claim: The maximum permissible amount for a single hospitalization claim for Optima Secure is ₹30 lakh, while for Optima Super Secure, it is ₹40 lakh.

6. Maximum permissible amount for all hospitalization claims in a policy year: The maximum permissible amount for all hospitalization claims in a policy year for Optima Secure is ₹40 lakh, while for Optima Super Secure, it is ₹50 lakh.

7. The other features, such as the coverage for expenses incurred on hospitalization due to Illness or Accident, the Secure Benefit that offers additional coverage amount equivalent to 100%/200% of the Base Sum Insured, the Plus Benefit that offers additional coverage equivalent to 100% of the Base Sum Insured in 2 years irrespective of a claim, the Automatic Restore Benefits that restores 100% of Base Sum Insured automatically on partial or complete utilization of Sum Insured, and the Protect Benefit that pays towards the Non-Medical expenses like gloves, food charges and other consumables during hospitalization, are common to both Optima Secure and Optima Super Secure plans.

QUESTION: How to file reimbursement claims? Give me step wise complete procedure.
ANSWER: To file reimbursement claims, follow these steps as specified in the policy text:

1. Submit the necessary documents to the Company within the prescribed time limit. (Section 11.6)
   Claim Types and Prescribed Time Limits:
   a. Reimbursement of Hospitalization, Day Care Treatment, or Pre-Hospitalization Expenses: Must be submitted within 30 days from the date of discharge from the hospital.
   b. Reimbursement of Post-Hospitalization Expenses: Must be submitted within 15 days from the completion of post-hospitalization treatment.

2. The reimbursement claim is to be supported with the following documents: (Section 11.7)
   a. Duly Completed claim form
   b. Photo ID and Age Proof
   c. Copy of the Hospital's Registration Certificate/Hospital Registration number in case of Hospitalization in any non Network Provider of the Company or certificate from Hospital authorities providing facilities available including number of beds.
   d. Discharge Card / Day Care Summary / Transfer Summary
   e. Final Hospital bill with all original deposit and final payment receipt and refund receipt(s), if advance amount refunded.
   f. Invoice with payment receipt and implant stickers for all implants used during Surgeries e.g. lens sticker and invoice in cataract Surgery, stent invoice and sticker in Angioplasty Surgery.
   g. All previous consultation papers indicating history and treatment details for current.

3. For claims outside India of Insured Person and Accompanying Person (as applicable), additional documents are required: (Section 11.7.1)
   a. Passport copy with entry and exit stamps
   b. Flight Tickets and Boarding Pass, if applicable
   c. Accommodation Invoices, if applicable
   d. Written advice from the overseas treating Medical Practitioner for requirement of an accompanying person during treatment.

4. Submit any other relevant document required by the Company for assessment of the claim. (Section 11.7.2)

5. The Company shall only accept bills/invoices/medical treatment related documents only in the Insured Person's name for whom the claim is submitted. (Section 11.7.2)

6. In the event of a claim lodged under the Policy and the original documents having been submitted to any other insurer, the Company shall accept the copy of the documents and claim settlement advice, duly certified by the other insurer, subject to satisfaction of the Company. (Section 11.7.2)

7. If requested by the Company, at the Company's cost, the Insured Person must submit to medical examination by Medical Practitioner appointed by the Company as often as it is considered reasonable and necessary and Company's representatives must be permitted to inspect the medical and Hospitalization records pertaining to the Insured Person's treatment, and to investigate the circumstances pertaining to the claim. (Section 11.7.2)

8. Any delay in notification or submission may be condoned on merit where delay is proved to be for reasons beyond the control of the Insured Person. (Section 12.2)




QUESTION:  What is the procedure for cashless claims in India? Give me step wise complete procedure.
ANSWER: The procedure for cashless claims in India, as specified in the policy text, is as follows:

1. The Insured Person can seek treatment at a Network Provider, but the treatment is subject to pre-authorization by the Company.
2. A cashless request form is available with the Network Provider.
3. The Network Provider shall obtain relevant information from the Insured Person/Policyholder and send a Cashless Facility request to the Company for authorization.
4. The Company, upon receiving the cashless request form and related medical information from the Insured Person/Network Provider, shall issue a pre-authorization letter to the Network Provider after verification.
5. At the time of discharge, the Insured Person shall verify and sign the discharge papers along with the final bill, pay for non-medical and inadmissible expenses.
6. In case the Insured Person is unable to provide the relevant medical details, the Company reserves the right to deny pre-authorization.
7. If cashless access is denied, the Insured Person may obtain treatment as per the treating doctor's advice and submit the claim documents to the Company for reimbursement.
8. The pre-authorization issued by the Company shall be valid for 15 days from the date of issuance (or expiry of the Policy, whichever is earlier).
9. The Company shall make payment for the Cashless facility to the authorized amount, directly to the Network Provider.

QUESTION:  What are the exact inclusions for post-hospitalization expenses? Give complete details.
ANSWER: The exact inclusions for post-hospitalization expenses, as per the provided policy text, are as follows:

1. Medical Expenses incurred by the Insured Person, related to an admissible Hospitalization under Section 3.1 (Hospitalization Expenses), for up to 180 days from the date of discharge from the Hospital. This includes:
   a. Room Rent, boarding, nursing expenses as provided by the Hospital / Nursing Home.
   b. Intensive Care Unit (ICU) / Intensive Cardiac Care Unit (ICCU) expenses.
   c. Surgeon, anaesthetist, Medical Practitioner, consultants, specialist Fees during Hospitalization forming part of Hospital bill.
   d. Investigative treatments and diagnostic procedures directly related to Hospitalization.
   e. Medicines and drugs prescribed in writing by Medical Practitioner.
   f. Intravenous fluids, blood transfusion, surgical appliances, allowable consumables and/or enteral feedings.
   g. Operation theatre charges.
   h. The cost of prosthetics and other devices or equipment, if implanted internally during Surgery.
   i. Expenses incurred on road Ambulance if the Insured Person is required to be transferred to the nearest Hospital for Emergency Care or from one Hospital to another Hospital or from Hospital to Home (within same city) following Hospitalization.
   j. In patient Care Dental Treatment, necessitated due to disease or Injury.
   k. Plastic surgery, necessitated due to Injury.
   l. All Day Care Treatments.

2. Non-Medical Expenses listed under Annexure B to this Policy, incurred in relation to a claim admissible under Section 3 (Base Coverage), during the Policy Year. However, the exclusion (k) of Section 10.2 - Specific Exclusions shall not apply to this Cover.

3. The provision or fitting of hearing aids, spectacles or contact lenses is not included in post-hospitalization expenses, as per the policy text.

4. Any treatment or part of a treatment that is not of a reasonable charge and not Medically Necessary, drugs or treatments which are not supported by a prescription, and any permanent exclusion applied on any medical or physical condition or treatment of an Insured Person as specifically mentioned in the Policy Schedule and as specifically accepted by Policyholder/Insured Person are also excluded from post-hospitalization expenses.

5. The post-hospitalization expenses coverage does not include expenses for alopecia, baldness including corticosteroids and topical immunotherapy wigs, toupees, hair pieces, any non-surgical hair replacement methods, optometric therapy, Artificial limbs and/or device used for diagnosis or treatment (except when used intra-operatively), prosthesis, corrective devices external durable medical equipment of any kind, wheelchairs, crutches, and oxygen concentrator for bronchial asthma/ COPD conditions, cost of cochlear implant(s) unless necessitated by an Accident.

6. The post-hospitalization expenses coverage does not include the organ donor's Pre-Hospitalization Expenses and Post Hospitalization Expenses, expenses related to organ transportation or preservation, and any other Medical Expenses or Hospitalization consequent to the organ harvesting.

7. The post-hospitalization expenses coverage is subject to the conditions specified in Section 3.6 of the policy.
